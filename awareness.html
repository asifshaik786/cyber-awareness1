Common Online Scams

Six scams responsible for most cyber fraud against rural families — with real examples, warning signs, and prevention tips for each.

📞

OTP Fraud

OTP మోసం
High Risk

A caller pretends to be from your bank, claims your account will be blocked, and asks you to read out the OTP sent to your phone to 'verify' or 'cancel' the block.

REAL EXAMPLE

"Sir, this is SBI security department. Your account shows suspicious activity and will be blocked in 10 minutes. Please share the OTP just sent to you to cancel the block." The victim shared it — ₹40,000 was withdrawn within a minute.

⚠ Warning Signs

  • Caller creates urgency or panic
  • Asks you to read out an OTP
  • Claims to be from a bank or government office

✅ Prevention Tips

  • Banks NEVER call asking for OTP
  • Hang up and call your bank's official number
  • OTP is only for actions YOU initiate
💳

UPI Fraud

UPI మోసం
Very Common

A scammer sends a 'collect request' on GPay/PhonePe/Paytm claiming you'll receive money. Approving it and entering your PIN actually sends your money to them.

REAL EXAMPLE

A buyer on OLX said "I'm sending ₹7,000 for your phone via GPay — just accept the request and enter your PIN." The victim did, and ₹7,000 was deducted from their own account instead.

⚠ Warning Signs

  • Any screen during a 'receive' asks for PIN
  • Stranger insists you act quickly
  • Buyer/seller you've never met in person

✅ Prevention Tips

  • Receiving money never requires a PIN
  • If asked for PIN, you are sending — reject it
  • Never share UPI PIN with anyone
💼

Fake Job Scams

నకిలీ ఉద్యోగ మోసాలు
Growing Fast

Messages offer easy work-from-home jobs with high daily pay. After collecting a 'registration fee', scammers ask for more fees, then disappear.

REAL EXAMPLE

"Data entry job! ₹800/day, no experience needed. Pay ₹299 registration to start." The victim paid, was asked for more "software" and "ID card" fees, then got blocked — total loss ₹3,797 with no job ever existing.

⚠ Warning Signs

  • Job asks for money before you start
  • Unrealistically high pay for simple work
  • No formal interview or company website

✅ Prevention Tips

  • Real employers pay you — never the reverse
  • Verify the company online before applying
  • Never pay a fee to 'get' a job
💬

WhatsApp Scams

WhatsApp మోసాలు
Common

A message from an unknown number claims to be a relative or friend with a new number, urgently asking for money to handle an 'emergency'.

REAL EXAMPLE

"Hi Dad, this is my new number, my phone broke. I urgently need ₹15,000 for a hospital bill, please send via GPay, I'll explain later." No such emergency existed — it was a scammer impersonating a family member.

⚠ Warning Signs

  • Unknown number claiming to be family
  • Urgent request for money, avoids phone calls
  • Asks you to keep it secret from others

✅ Prevention Tips

  • Call the person's known number to verify
  • Be suspicious of any new-number claims
  • Never send money on a text request alone
📲

Fake Loan Apps

నకిలీ లోన్ యాప్‌లు
Dangerous

Unregistered lending apps offer instant loans with minimal paperwork. They require access to your contacts and photos, then use harassment to recover money at extreme interest rates.

REAL EXAMPLE

A victim took a ₹5,000 instant loan from an app found through a Facebook ad. Within a week, the app demanded ₹12,000 in repayment and began messaging the victim's entire contact list with morphed photos to pressure payment.

⚠ Warning Signs

  • App asks for contacts/gallery access
  • No RBI registration or physical address
  • Extremely high interest, hidden charges

✅ Prevention Tips

  • Only use RBI-registered lenders (check rbi.org.in)
  • Never grant contacts/gallery permissions to loan apps
  • Prefer banks or known NBFCs
📸

Social Media Fraud

సోషల్ మీడియా మోసం
All Ages

Fake profiles on Facebook or Instagram send friend requests, build a relationship over weeks, then ask for personal photos or money — later used for blackmail.

REAL EXAMPLE

A stranger messaged "I found you on Facebook, you seem nice, let's be friends" and chatted for weeks before asking for a personal photo. The photo was later used to demand money under threat of sharing it publicly.

⚠ Warning Signs

  • Stranger asking for photos or personal info
  • Relationship moves very fast
  • Asks to keep the friendship secret

✅ Prevention Tips

  • Set your profile to private
  • Never share personal photos with online-only contacts
  • If threatened, report immediately at cybercrime.gov.in
